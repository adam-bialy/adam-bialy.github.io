Chemical research

I have been involved with chemistry as a topic of study and research for 15 years. In 2018 I obtained a PhD degree in chemical sciences at University of Warsaw. My work has been focused on compounds of silver and their potential application as novel high-temperature superconductors. As part of Prof. Wojciech Grochala's team at University of Warsaw's Center of New Technologies, I have approached this area from a variety of angles, including photoelecton spectroscopy, high-pressure crystallography and—in recent years—computational modelling of new hypothetical silver and hydride-based materials. As of April 2022, I have written or contributed to 18 scientific papers, a complete list of which can be found on Google Scholar.
My personal highlight: our visionary 2020 paper in Physical Review Materials, which was distinguished as Editor's Choice by the journal.

Programming

In parallel to my research career, I have pursued coding both as a tool for data analysis in my scientific work, and as a hobby. I have a working knowledge of Python, including data science libraries such as numpy, pandas and matplotlib. I am also interested in machine learning and neural networks. I am familiar with principles of statistical learning methods and deep learning, and with corresponding tools implemented in Python (scikit-learn and tensorflow) and R. Other programming skills in my toolkit include front-end development with HTML/CSS/Javascript, and basic knowledge of SQL and C#. My coding projects can be found on my GitHub profile.
Example applications: hearing test and neural network for simple bird recognition (Corvidae).

Science communication

Throughout my professional life, I have had many opportunities to discuss science with diverse groups of people and using different means of communication. I worked as an explainer at Copernicus Science Center in Warsaw, where I would engage in conversations with visitors in order to enhance their experience and explain scientific topics to them in an accessible manner. I have written many scientific articles for specialized journals and delivered talks at international conferences. During my PhD studies, I taught and assisted students in laboratory classes. During the COVID-19 pandemic, I worked at Pfizer as a medical information consultant, responding to queries made by healthcare professionals and patients.
